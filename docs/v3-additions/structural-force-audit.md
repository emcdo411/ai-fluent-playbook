## The Framing Fix

Current framing: These are mindset challenges the individual needs to overcome.
Correct framing: These are organizational structural conditions the individual cannot
resolve alone. The tools are Ch. 9 and Ch. 12, not the mindset sections.

Recommended language for Ch. 5 or Introduction:
If you are finding it hard to apply this playbook's guidance despite genuinely wanting
to - if the sprint feels too slow, if experimenting publicly feels unsafe, if being
visibly learning creates status risk in your team - the problem is almost certainly
not your mindset. It is your organization's structural conditions.
Chapters 9 and 12 are written specifically for this situation. Start there.

## Identity Crystallization - The Avoidant Reader Fix

Snowflake Equation Lab v4 Identity Crystallization: the terminal stage where a
threatened identity becomes so rigid that new information confirms rather than
resolves the threat.

Applied: Practitioners with a self-protective narrative around AI is overhyped and
I am fine may read You are not late as validation of avoidance, not a call to action.

Fix - add to the Prologue before the reassurance:

Option A (Direct):
If you picked up this playbook partly to confirm that you do not need it - that the AI
disruption is overhyped and your current approach is fine - stay with that for one more
page. You may be right. Or there may be a gap between the signal and the story you are
telling yourself about it. Either way, the evidence is in Chapter 2.

Option B (Lighter):
This playbook is not written for people who are certain they do not need it.
If you are in that camp, Chapter 2 is the relevant chapter - not the Prologue.
