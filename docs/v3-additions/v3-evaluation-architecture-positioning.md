## What Evaluation Architecture Is

Organizations with identical AI tool access, talent, and budget show dramatically
different productivity outcomes from AI adoption. The variable that explains the gap
is not the tools — it is the quality of the evaluation infrastructure surrounding
those tools: how teams decide when to use AI, when not to, what counts as good
AI-assisted output, and who owns the consequences when AI gets it wrong.

The playbook builds exactly this infrastructure. The Agent Role Definition Canvas,
HITL handoff design, governance scaffolding, and 30-Day Sprint are all evaluation
architecture components — they define the criteria by which human-AI collaboration
is judged and governed.

## Competitive Moat Framing

Teams that complete the 30-Day AI Fluency Sprint do not just use AI better
individually — they establish shared evaluation criteria, governance scaffolding,
and human-AI handoff standards that function as competitive infrastructure. Once
institutionalized, these standards are genuinely difficult for competitors to replicate
quickly. The moat is the judgment system, not the tool stack.
