## Fix 1: Pathway 1 - Scope Encoding Disclosure
Where: Introduction or first page of Part 1

The issue: The playbook encodes an agile-native, software-product-centric,
English-language professional context as the universal reference without acknowledgment.

Recommended language:
A note on scope: this playbook is calibrated to agile-native, software product
environments. Practitioners in regulated industries (pharma, medical device, financial
services), hardware product contexts, government product roles, or non-English-speaking
markets should adapt the governance and tool recommendations to their specific constraints.
The core thesis applies broadly. The specific frameworks are optimized for one context.

---

## Fix 2: Pathway 2 - Structural Context Sidebar
Where: Chapter 9 (Psychological Safety) or Introduction

The issue: The AI-fluent professional competency criteria correlate with specific
personality configurations and psychologically safe org cultures. Workers in low-safety
environments face structural barriers - not individual capability gaps.

Recommended language:
A note on structural context: the behaviors described in this playbook are most
accessible in psychologically safe, experimentation-positive organizations. If your
organization does not yet have that culture - if experimenting publicly carries career
risk, if admitting uncertainty signals weakness, if being behind on AI creates status
anxiety - the problem is not your mindset. It is your organization's structural
conditions. Chapters 9 and 12 are written specifically for this situation.

## Fix 3: Pathway 3 - Research Provenance Note
Where: Displacement Risk sidebar (Priority 2) or footnote

The issue: The augmentation narrative is cited as structural evidence without flagging
that the primary research sources are commercially invested in that narrative prevailing.

Recommended language:
It is worth noting that the largest studies supporting the augmentation is winning
narrative come disproportionately from the organizations most commercially invested in
that narrative. This does not make the findings wrong - the evidence is directionally
sound. It is a reason to read the research with appropriate provenance awareness.
