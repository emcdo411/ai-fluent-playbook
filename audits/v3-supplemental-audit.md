## Module B: Evaluation Architecture Analysis

The playbook is an evaluation architecture upgrade tool - it produces practitioners
capable of designing better human-AI evaluation handoffs and building governance
standards. Tim has written an evaluation architecture upgrade product and positioned
it as a skills playbook. These are not the same market.
See docs/v3-additions/ec-layer-positioning.md

Evaluation Architecture Moat:
Add to Ch. 1: The practitioners and teams who build strong AI evaluation architectures
earliest will hold a structural competitive advantage - not because of the tools they
use, but because of the judgment systems they have institutionalized.

Capability Criteria Decay - Escalation:
The governance criteria themselves (HITL handoffs, agent role definitions) will decay
discontinuously at the next generation boundary - not just Appendix B tool recommendations.
The Capability Horizon Clause must cover governance criteria, not just tool landscape.

Pre-Publication Window:
Tim is pre-publication - the highest-leverage remediation window. All three research
bias corrections are parameter-level adjustments, not structural redesigns.

## Module C: Structural Force Audit

Three structural forces block implementation - not individual skill or willingness:
1. Speed Pressure Override: org-level velocity demands short-circuit HITL governance
2. Psychological Safety Deficit: low-safety teams cannot safely experiment
3. AI Competence Visibility Risk: status risk inhibits public learning posture

All three are currently framed as individual mindset challenges. They should be named
as organizational structural conditions. Ch. 9 and Ch. 12 are the remedies.
See docs/v3-additions/structural-force-audit.md

Avoidant Reader Risk:
Practitioners with a self-protective narrative around AI being overhyped may read
'You are not late' as validation of avoidance rather than a call to action. A pattern
disruption move before the reassurance would reach this reader type.
See docs/v3-additions/structural-force-audit.md
